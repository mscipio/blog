About me...

I'm a biomedical engineer, but also an IT enthusiast (currently involved in a PhD program in biomedical engineering, you can see my CV clicking here) from the UNIPI - University of Pisa, Pisa, Italy. I'm also a TA in Immunology courses at U.N.L.P. and I taught biostatistics courses at the Colegio de Bioquímicos - Colegio Zonal XII - Buenos Aires. I'm a member of Python Argentina (PyAr) [1] and Quantitative Finance Club (QFClub) [2].

My main focus is the quantitative financial time series research and analysis using Python as the main computational tool. But, I also develop several data science, computer vision, and IPython-related projects.

Recently, I have presented talks and posters in several international and national conferences:

  • PyConUS 2012 ([3 - 6]), SciPy 2013 ([7 - 10]).
  • PyDay Lujan 2012 [11], PyConAr 2011 ([12 - 13]), PyConAr 2012 ([14 - 15]), SciPyConAr 2013 ([16 - 19]).

In the open development area, I can list the following projects:

  • IPython plugin for NINJA-IDE, which gives IPython notebook support to this IDE (written and focused in Python) [20].
  • vIPer (based in the plugin described before), an independent application with several features focused to the visualization and publication of the IPython notebooks [21].

and also some contributions to other projects:

  • IPython project, with development ideas, specially in the nbconvert repository, providing support for the generation of static html-slideshows from IPython notebooks [22 - 23(demo)].
  • Nikola, a static site generator, providing support for publishing posts with IPython notebooks, a "smart" mathjax loader and a new reveal.js-based theme [24 - 25].
